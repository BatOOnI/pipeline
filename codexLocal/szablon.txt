<h2><span style="color: #9fa0ab;"><strong>Built Properly from the Ground Up</strong></span></h2>
[/fusion_text][fusion_content_boxes layout="clean-horizontal" columns="1" heading_size="2" box_shadow="no" box_shadow_blur="0" box_shadow_spread="0" iconspin="no" icon_align="left" animation_direction="left" animation_speed="0.3" animation_delay="0" hide_on_mobile="small-visibility,medium-visibility,large-visibility" body_color="var(--awb-color5)" backgroundcolor="var(--awb-color5)" circlecolor="var(--awb-color4)" circlebordersize="0" outercirclebordersize="0" icon_size="15"][fusion_content_box title="Reinforced slab foundations" linktext="Read More" animation_direction="left" animation_speed="0.3" icon="fa-arrow-right fas" backgroundcolor="var(--awb-color6)"]

<span style="color: #9fa0ab;">Every proper garden room starts with the base. Our builds are constructed on <strong data-start="4041" data-end="4081">reinforced concrete slab foundations</strong>, not lightweight pads or cheap temporary bases. That gives the structure proper support, better long-term stability and a stronger platform for the whole build.
</span>

[/fusion_content_box][fusion_content_box title="Raised, ventilated floor construction" linktext="Read More" animation_direction="left" animation_speed="0.3" icon="fa-arrow-right fas" backgroundcolor="var(--awb-color6)"]

<span style="color: #9fa0ab;">Our floors are raised off the slab base and ventilated. That matters. It helps protect the structure, improves durability and reduces the risk of long-term moisture-related problems. Combined with proper membranes and insulation, this creates a far more robust garden building than many prefabricated units.</span>

[/fusion_content_box][fusion_content_box title="Treated structural timber" linktext="Read More" animation_direction="left" animation_speed="0.3" icon="fa-arrow-right fas" backgroundcolor="var(--awb-color6)"]

The timber we use is structural, treated and impregnated for long-term performance. These buildings are not designed as throwaway garden units. They are built to withstand the weather and to last in a way that is much closer to <a href="https://btomconstruction.co.uk/house-extensions-southend/">conventional building standards</a>.

<h2 data-start="4915" data-end="4963"><span style="color: #ff7351;"><strong data-start="4915" data-end="4963">Insulation, Membranes and Year-Round Comfort</strong></span></h2>

<h2 data-start="5952" data-end="5989"><span style="color: #ff7351;"><strong data-start="5952" data-end="5989">Why We Use Cold Roof Construction</strong></span></h2>
<p data-start="5991" data-end="6096">All of these projects were built with a <strong data-start="6031" data-end="6044">cold roof</strong> design. For many clients, that is the right choice.</p>
<p data-start="6098" data-end="6739">A cold roof helps keep the overall building height lower, which can be important when working close to boundaries or when trying to stay within permitted development limits. In practical terms, that often means a client can build a garden room without needing a full planning application, depending on the exact site conditions and dimensions. In England, outbuildings commonly fall under permitted development, but height limits are key: non-dual-pitched roofs are usually capped at <strong data-start="6582" data-end="6597">3 m overall</strong>, and if the outbuilding is within <strong data-start="6632" data-end="6653">2 m of a boundary</strong>, total height is typically capped at <strong data-start="6691" data-end="6700">2.5 m</strong>.</p>
<p data-start="6741" data-end="7023">Cold roof construction is also easier to maintain and usually more cost-effective than a warm roof, while still allowing us to keep a strong insulation specification. On our builds, the roof is typically insulated to around <strong data-start="6965" data-end="6975">150 mm</strong>, helping deliver proper all-season performance.</p>
<p data-start="7025" data-end="7313">For roof covering, we usually use a<a href="https://btomconstruction.co.uk/general-building-repairs-southend/"> <strong data-start="7061" data-end="7097">high-quality felt roofing system</strong></a> with multiple underlay and top layers. When installed properly, these systems can offer manufacturer-backed warranties of around <strong data-start="7227" data-end="7239">25 years</strong>, which gives clients confidence in the long-term durability of the build.</p>

<h2 data-start="7327" data-end="7368"><span style="color: #ff7351;"><strong data-start="7327" data-end="7368">Drainage and Ground Conditions Matter</strong></span></h2>
<p data-start="7370" data-end="7437">A garden room is only as good as the ground and drainage around it.</p>
<p data-start="7439" data-end="7856">On some sites, especially where the surrounding ground sits higher than the slab or where water naturally runs toward the building, proper drainage planning is essential. <h2 data-start="12200" data-end="12266"><span style="color: #ff7351;"><strong data-start="12200" data-end="12266">Do You Need Planning Permission for a Garden Room in Southend?</strong></span></h2>
<p data-start="12268" data-end="12385">In many cases, a garden room can be built under <strong data-start="12316" data-end="12341">permitted development</strong>, but it depends on the site and the design.</p>
<p data-start="12387" data-end="12427">In England, key rules typically include:</p>

<ul data-start="12428" data-end="12839">
 	<li data-start="12428" data-end="12468">the building must be <strong data-start="12451" data-end="12468">single storey</strong></li>
 	<li data-start="12469" data-end="12572">overall height is usually limited to <strong data-start="12508" data-end="12540">4 m with a dual-pitched roof</strong> or <strong data-start="12544" data-end="12572">3 m for other roof types</strong></li>
 	<li data-start="12573" data-end="12670">if the building is within <strong data-start="12601" data-end="12622">2 m of a boundary</strong>, it usually must not exceed <strong data-start="12651" data-end="12660">2.5 m</strong> in height</li>
 	<li data-start="12671" data-end="12748">it must not take up more than <strong data-start="12703" data-end="12748">50% of the land around the original house</strong></li>
 	<li data-start="12749" data-end="12839">it must not be a separate self-contained dwelling.</li>
</ul>
<p data-start="12841" data-end="12926">That is one reason our lower-profile <strong data-start="12878" data-end="12891">cold roof</strong> builds are often a smart solution.</p>
<p data-start="12928" data-end="13288">Building regulations are a separate issue. Small detached outbuildings are often exempt, but the exact position can depend on size, boundary distance, materials and use. If the room is intended as incidental accommodation such as an office, gym or studio, that is generally simpler than a fully self-contained living unit.</p>
